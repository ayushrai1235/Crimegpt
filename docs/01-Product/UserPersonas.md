# User Personas

## Overview
The **User Personas** document defines the archetypal users of the **CrimeGPT** platform. Understanding these personas is critical for designing an intuitive user interface and ensuring that the system architecture—powered by **Zoho Catalyst**—meets the specific performance and security needs of each rank within the Karnataka State Police (KSP).

## 1. Inspector "Ramesh" (Station House Officer - SHO)

**Role:** Inspector in charge of a high-traffic urban police station (e.g., Indiranagar, Bengaluru).
**Age:** 45 | **Tech Savviness:** Medium (Uses WhatsApp and basic web apps daily; dislikes complex legacy government software).

**Goals:**
- Maintain law and order within his specific jurisdiction.
- Deploy limited patrol vehicles (Hoysalas) efficiently.
- Quickly brief his superiors on major incidents.

**Pain Points:**
- Spends too much time reading through physical files to brief the morning shift.
- Patrol routes are often based on "gut feeling" rather than data.
- Struggles to track habitual offenders who operate just outside his jurisdiction.

**How CrimeGPT Helps (Use Cases):**
- **Predictive Dashboards:** Ramesh logs in via **Catalyst Authentication**. The dashboard, instantly loaded via **Catalyst Cache**, shows a heatmap of predicted chain-snatching hotspots for the upcoming weekend.
- **Automated Briefings:** He clicks a button to generate a PDF briefing for his patrol teams. A **Catalyst Function** pulls data, generates the PDF, saves it to the **Catalyst File Store**, and provides a download link.

## 2. Sub-Inspector "Priya" (Investigating Officer - IO)

**Role:** Lead investigator assigned to specific major crimes (e.g., a string of organized burglaries).
**Age:** 32 | **Tech Savviness:** High (Comfortable with modern web applications and basic data analysis tools).

**Goals:**
- Solve assigned cases quickly and secure high-quality evidence for chargesheets.
- Identify accomplices and trace the Modus Operandi (M.O.).
- Build airtight cases that lead to convictions.

**Pain Points:**
- It takes days to request and receive records from neighboring police stations.
- Finding connections between suspects (e.g., discovering two suspects were previously cellmates) requires manual cross-referencing.
- Fear of missing a critical piece of evidence buried in a 100-page historical FIR.

**How CrimeGPT Helps (Use Cases):**
- **Conversational Intelligence:** Priya uses the chat interface to ask, *"Has this M.O. (breaking through the roof) been used in Mysore recently?"*
- **Explainable AI:** The system returns an answer and provides a direct link to a PDF in the **Catalyst File Store**. Priya can trust the AI because it provides proof.
- **Criminal Network Analysis:** A **Catalyst Function** queries the Neo4j database and displays a visual graph showing how her primary suspect is financially linked to a known gang leader.

## 3. Analyst "Suresh" (State Crime Records Bureau - SCRB)

**Role:** Data analyst stationed at the state headquarters, responsible for identifying macro trends.
**Age:** 50 | **Tech Savviness:** Very High (Comfortable writing complex SQL queries and creating statistical models).

**Goals:**
- Identify emerging crime trends across the entire state before they become crises.
- Provide statistical reports to the Commissioner and government officials.
- Maintain the integrity of the state's crime data.

**Pain Points:**
- Existing databases (CCTNS) are slow and difficult to extract macro-level data from without specialized DB admin support.
- Generating the annual crime report takes months of manual data aggregation.

**How CrimeGPT Helps (Use Cases):**
- **Advanced Search:** Suresh uses the advanced filtering UI powered by **Catalyst Search** to instantly query millions of records in the **Catalyst Data Store**.
- **Trend Forecasting:** He relies on the sociological insights generated by background **Catalyst Cron** jobs to correlate crime spikes with external factors like unemployment or seasonal migrations.

---

## 4. Commissioner "Vikram" (Executive Leadership)

**Role:** Top-tier leadership responsible for overall state security and policy.
**Age:** 55 | **Tech Savviness:** Low to Medium (Relies on subordinates for data extraction; needs high-level, easy-to-read dashboards).

**Goals:**
- Ensure public safety and maintain high conviction rates.
- Allocate state budget and resources effectively.
- Answer to the government and media regarding crime statistics.

**Pain Points:**
- Reports are often outdated by the time they reach his desk.
- Lack of real-time visibility into the performance of individual police stations.

**How CrimeGPT Helps (Use Cases):**
- **Macro Dashboards:** Vikram logs in and sees a state-wide, real-time dashboard. The UI is simple and focuses only on high-level KPIs (crime rates up/down, pending major cases).
- **Executive Summaries:** He can ask CrimeGPT high-level questions like, *"What is the primary cause of the recent spike in cybercrime in Hubli?"* and receive a synthesized, cited summary.
